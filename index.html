Geology is the science that studies the Earth and its history. It investigates the structure, composition, and evolution of the planet and its natural resources. Geology also explores the interactions between the Earth and other celestial bodies, such as the Moon and Mars.

Geology is divided into many branches and subdisciplines, depending on the methods and topics of study. Some of the main branches are mineralogy, petrology, geochemistry, geophysics, structural geology, stratigraphy, paleontology, geomorphology, and planetary geology. Each branch applies different techniques and theories to understand various aspects of the Earth and its environment.

Geology is a broad and interdisciplinary field that encompasses many subfields and specialties. Some of the main subfields are volcanology, paleontology, hydrology, seismology, geomorphology, and economic geology.
